02 · The Problem

Why 90% of People Get Terrible AI Results

They treat AI like a search engine. It isn't one. It's a reasoning partner — and reasoning partners need context.

❌ How most people prompt "Write a report on climate change" "Give me a presentation" "Help me with my email" "Write a business plan"
✅ How professionals should prompt "Write a 3-page executive summary on climate change impacts in Southern Africa for a Botswana government audience, formal tone, include policy recommendations, cite 3 specific risks" "Create 8 slides for a 15-minute pitch to retail investors. Include problem, solution, market size (Africa), business model, traction, team, financials, ask"
🎯

The Core Insight

AI doesn't know your context, your audience, your constraints, or your standards. You do. A better prompt is not about being clever — it's about transferring your professional expertise into the AI's context window before asking for output. The more context you give, the closer the first draft is to what you actually need.

03 · The Core Framework

The CRAFT Prompting Framework

Five elements. Every single one makes the output better. Together they transform results.

The CRAFT Formula

C — Context
+
R — Role
+
A — Action
+
F — Format
+
T — Tone/Target

C — Context

What is the situation? What does AI need to understand about your world before it can help?

Example
"I am the HR Director at a 200-person manufacturing company in Gaborone, Botswana. We are onboarding 40 new employees next month..."

R — Role

What expert perspective should AI adopt? Assigning a role dramatically shifts the quality of reasoning.

Example
"Act as a senior HR consultant who specialises in African workplace culture and employee experience..."

A — Action

What exactly do you need? One specific, unambiguous action verb. Not "help me" — tell AI precisely what to produce.

Example
"Write a 5-day onboarding schedule with daily objectives, activities, and responsible persons..."

F — Format

How should the output look? Table, bullet points, numbered steps, paragraph, email, script, slide deck — specify it.

Example
"Format as a day-by-day table with columns: Day, Theme, Morning Activity, Afternoon Activity, Outcome..."

T — Tone & Target

Who is reading this? What tone fits? Professional, casual, encouraging, technical, simple — and what does the reader already know?

Example
"Tone: welcoming but professional. Target audience: new employees, mix of school leavers and experienced professionals. Avoid jargon. Include a cultural welcome note recognising Botswana's Botho values."

🎯 Full CRAFT Prompt — In Action

Complete CRAFT Prompt
CONTEXT: I am the HR Director at a 200-person manufacturing company in Gaborone, Botswana. We are onboarding 40 new employees next month — a mix of recent graduates and experienced hires.

ROLE: Act as a senior HR consultant who specialises in African workplace culture and employee experience.

ACTION: Write a 5-day onboarding schedule with daily objectives, activities, and responsible persons.

FORMAT: Format as a day-by-day table with columns: Day, Theme, Morning Activity, Afternoon Activity, Key Outcome.

TONE/TARGET: Professional but welcoming. Include a cultural touchpoint each day that reflects Botho values. Avoid corporate jargon.
04 · Advanced Techniques

6 Techniques That Separate Power Users

01

Chain-of-Thought Prompting

Ask AI to think through a problem step by step before giving you the answer. Critical for analysis, strategy, and decisions where the reasoning matters as much as the conclusion.

Trigger Phrase
"Think through this step by step before giving me your recommendation: [problem]"
"Before answering, lay out the key considerations, then give your conclusion."

Use when: Strategic decisions, risk analysis, problem diagnosis, ethical questions

02

Few-Shot Examples

Give AI 2–3 examples of what "good" looks like before asking for the output. The AI calibrates its output to match your examples' style, structure, and standard.

Structure
"Here are two examples of the kind of email I write:
EXAMPLE 1: [paste example]
EXAMPLE 2: [paste example]
Now write a similar email to [recipient] about [topic]."

Use when: You want AI to match your voice, a specific format, or a quality standard you've already set

03

Constraint Framing

Set explicit rules and limits. Nothing sharpens AI output like specific constraints — word limits, things to avoid, things to include, structure requirements.

Example
"CONSTRAINTS:
- Maximum 150 words
- Do not mention price
- Must include one Africa-specific example
- End with a clear call to action
- Avoid the word 'leverage'"

Use when: Writing for specific channels (LinkedIn, email), compliance content, anything with quality rules

04

Devil's Advocate Prompting

Force AI to challenge your plan. This technique is underused by executives — it is one of the most valuable things AI can do for decision-making.

Example
"Here is my business plan for [X]. Your job is not to encourage me — it is to steelman the case against this plan. Give me the 5 strongest reasons this will fail. Be brutal."

Use when: Business plans, strategy, hiring decisions, investment proposals

05

Persona Stacking

Give AI multiple perspectives at once by asking it to respond from several roles simultaneously. Powerful for presentations and documents that need to serve multiple audiences.

Example
"Review this proposal from three perspectives:
1. A risk-averse CFO
2. An ambitious CEO
3. A sceptical board member
For each, give 2 concerns and 1 thing they'd find compelling."
06

Iterative Refinement

Most professionals stop after the first AI response. Power users iterate. Each follow-up refines the output toward exactly what they need.

Refinement Chain
Turn 1: [Initial CRAFT prompt]
Turn 2: "Good. Now make it 30% shorter and sharpen the opening sentence."
Turn 3: "Change the tone — it's too formal. Make it sound more like a conversation."
Turn 4: "Add a specific Botswana example in paragraph 2."

The first response is a starting point. The refined response is the professional output.

05 · Ready-to-Use Templates

Your Professional Prompt Library

Copy, adapt, and use. These are starting points — customise for your context.

📧 Professional Email

Template
Write a professional email to [recipient role] at [organisation]. Purpose: [one sentence]. Tone: [formal/collaborative/assertive]. Include: [specific points]. Length: [short/medium]. My name is [name], [role] at [org].

📊 Data Analysis Summary

Template
Analyse this data: [paste data]. I need: 3 key insights, 2 risks, and 1 recommendation. Audience: [who]. Format: executive summary, max 250 words. Highlight the most surprising finding first.

🎤 Presentation Script

Template
Write a [X] minute spoken presentation script on [topic] for [audience]. Include: opening hook, 3 key messages, 1 Africa-relevant example, strong close with call to action. Conversational, not stiff.

📋 Meeting Minutes

Template
Convert these rough notes into professional meeting minutes: [paste notes]. Include: date, attendees, agenda items, decisions made, action items (with owner and due date). Format: structured document.

💡 Problem-Solving

Template
I'm facing this challenge: [describe problem]. Context: [situation]. Constraints: [budget/time/resources]. Generate 5 solutions ranked by feasibility in a Botswana/African context. For each: pros, cons, first action step.

📱 Social Media Content

Template
Write 5 LinkedIn posts about [topic] for [professional audience]. Each post: 150–200 words, starts with a scroll-stopping opening line, ends with a question to drive engagement. Tone: [authoritative/conversational/inspiring].

This exercise shows you how much better output gets through iteration.

  1. Ask AI: "Write a short bio for me as a professional in [your field]." Paste the result below.
  2. Refine: "Good. Make it 40% shorter."
  3. Refine: "Now write it in first person, more conversational."
  4. Refine: "Add one specific achievement or credibility marker."
  5. Refine: "The ending is weak. Rewrite the last sentence to leave a stronger impression."
