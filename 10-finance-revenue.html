03 · The Money Section

Pricing Intelligence — Stop Undercharging

This section will make you uncomfortable. It should. Discomfort here usually means money is being left on the table.

💡

The Three Pricing Models — Know Which You're Using and Why

  • Cost-plus pricing: "It costs me X to produce, I add 30% margin." Simple, common, and almost always leaves money on the table.
  • Competitive pricing: "Others charge X, I'll charge Y." Anchors your value to competitors who may be undercharging too.
  • Value-based pricing: "The client gets W value from this — my price reflects that." The most profitable model. Requires you to quantify value — which is where AI helps.

🤖 AI-Assisted Value Quantification Prompt

Use this prompt to build your value-based pricing case. This single exercise has helped professionals increase their rates by 40–150%.

"I provide [your service] to [client type] in [industry/location].

Help me quantify the value I create. For each of these categories, estimate the annual financial value to a typical client:
1. Time saved (hours × their hourly rate or equivalent)
2. Revenue increased (if applicable)
3. Costs avoided (mistakes, inefficiencies, risk)
4. Competitive advantage gained
5. Peace of mind / risk reduction (estimate conservatively)

Based on this value calculation, what would be a defensible price range for my service?
Compare to my current rate of [your current rate]. Am I undercharging, and if so, by how much?"

05 · Critical Skill

AI-Assisted Cash Flow Forecasting

Cash flow kills more viable businesses than bad products. AI can spot trouble 60–90 days early — but only if you're feeding it data.

📥 What to Feed AI

  • Last 12 months of revenue (month by month)
  • Known upcoming expenses by month
  • Outstanding invoices + expected payment dates
  • Confirmed upcoming contracts
  • Fixed costs: rent, salaries, subscriptions
  • Seasonal patterns (if any)

📤 What AI Produces

  • Month-by-month cash position forecast
  • Low-cash warning months flagged in advance
  • "What if" scenarios (what if invoice X is late?)
  • Recommended buffer amount
  • Growth scenario: impact of adding one client
  • Summary report suitable for a bank or investor

Cash Flow Forecast Prompt

"Here is my financial data for the past 6 months: [paste monthly revenue and expense figures].

Known upcoming items:
- [expense/invoice] due [month]: [amount]
- [expense/invoice] due [month]: [amount]

Please:
1. Create a 3-month cash flow forecast table showing opening balance, inflows, outflows, and closing balance
2. Flag any months where cash may go below [your safety buffer, e.g. BWP 50,000]
3. Identify the single biggest cash flow risk in the next 90 days
4. Give me one action to reduce that risk

Assume payment terms: clients pay within 30 days of invoice."
Exercise 01 · Critical

💰 The Undercharging Audit

This exercise is confronting for most people. Do it anyway.

  1. List your top 3 products or services with their current prices.
  2. For each one, write one sentence describing the value it delivers to the client.
  3. Run the Value Quantification prompt (Section 03) for each product/service in ChatGPT or Claude.
  4. Compare AI's value estimate to your current price.
  5. Write the gap: "I am undercharging for [X] by approximately [amount/percentage]."

Exercise 02 · Practical

📊 Your First AI Financial Summary

Build the habit of letting AI interpret your numbers — not just store them.

  1. Open your last 3 months of invoices, bank statements, or income records.
  2. Summarise the numbers in a simple table: Month, Revenue, Main Expense, Net.
  3. Paste into ChatGPT or Claude with this request: "Analyse this data. Give me: top revenue source, biggest cost drain, one risk I might not be seeing, and one growth action for next month."
